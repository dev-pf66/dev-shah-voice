# Psychology Tactics Dev Uses

Mental models and psychological triggers baked into Dev's content.

---

## Contrast Principle

Show the before/after, the wrong vs right, the common belief vs truth.

**In practice:**
- "Most people think X. They're wrong."
- "$4K → six figures"
- "80 deals looked at. 2 closed."

Always create contrast between the expected and the reality.

---

## Specificity = Credibility

Specific numbers feel true. Round numbers feel made up.

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Many deals" | "47 deals" |
| "Made money" | "Made $127K" |
| "Took time" | "Took 6 months" |
| "A lot of people" | "2,847 members" |

**Dev's pattern:** Specific numbers in hooks, timeframes in outcomes.

---

## Social Proof Triggers

Dev uses social proof differently — not logos, but **peer signals**:
- "I get DMs every day asking..."
- "650+ new followers in 24 hours"
- "180K impressions on this thread"

Show the audience responding, not just brands endorsing.

---

## Mimetic Desire

People want what others want. Create desire by showing demand:
- Waitlists with counts
- "DMs are open" (implies people are DMing)
- Showing applications/interest ("3-5 people will get selected")

---

## IKEA Effect

People value what they participate in creating.

**In practice:**
- Ask questions that make readers think
- "What would you do differently?"
- Polls, threads asking for input
- Community co-creation

---

## Loss Aversion

People fear losing more than they desire gaining.

**Dev's framing:**
- "The deal I walked away from" (loss prevented)
- "What you're missing by not doing X"
- "This opportunity won't last" (scarcity)

---

## Mere Exposure Effect

Familiarity breeds trust. Show up consistently.

**Dev's approach:**
- Daily Twitter presence
- Recurring themes (micro PE, acquire.com, building in public)
- Signature phrases repeated across content

---

## The Underdog Effect

Root for the underdog. Dev positions as:
- "Just a 22-year-old"
- "Started with <$10K"
- "2.4 GPA"
- "Random kid on the internet"

This creates relatability and makes success feel achievable.

---

## Curiosity Gap

Open a loop the reader needs to close.

**Patterns:**
- "Here's what I learned:" (then deliver)
- "The real reason is..." (then reveal)
- "I was wrong about..." (then correct)

Never close the loop in the hook — make them read.

---

## Availability Heuristic

Recent, vivid examples feel more common/achievable.

**Dev's approach:**
- Real-time updates ("Last 24 hours...")
- Recent deals, recent numbers
- Fresh case studies, not old ones

Make success feel current and achievable, not historical.
